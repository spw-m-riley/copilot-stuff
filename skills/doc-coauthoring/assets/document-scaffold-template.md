# Document Structure Scaffolds

This is **optional guidance** to help you get started. These scaffolds show recommended section structures for common document types—think of them as starting templates. Your specific document may need different sections, and that's completely fine.

Use these as inspiration. During **Stage 2 refinement**, we'll work together to adjust the structure to fit your needs.

---

## Proposal / Product Requirements Document (PRD)

```
# [Document Title]

## Problem
## Goals
## Success Metrics
## Proposed Approach
## Timeline & Milestones
## Resources & Dependencies
## Risks & Mitigations
## Open Questions
```

**Best for**: Product proposals, new features, cross-team initiatives.

---

## Technical Specification

```
# [System/Feature Name] Technical Specification

## Overview & Context
## Goals & Non-Goals
## Architecture & Design
## API / Interface Specification
## Implementation Details
## Testing Strategy
## Rollout & Monitoring
## Open Questions & Future Work
```

**Best for**: System design, API contracts, architectural decisions.

---

## Decision Record / ADR (Architecture Decision Record)

```
# [ADR Title]

## Status
## Context
## Decision
## Rationale
## Consequences & Trade-offs
## Alternatives Considered
## Related Decisions
```

**Best for**: Recording "why we chose X over Y" decisions for future reference.

---

## RFC / Design Document

```
# [RFC Title]

## Motivation & Problem Statement
## Goals & Success Criteria
## Proposed Solution
## Detailed Design
## Alternative Approaches & Trade-offs
## Implementation Plan
## Risks & Mitigations
## Questions & Discussion Points
```

**Best for**: Open-ended design proposals, stakeholder collaboration, exploratory documents.

---

## Process / Runbook / Operational Guide

```
# [Process Name]

## Overview & Purpose
## Scope & Audience
## Prerequisites & Setup
## Step-by-Step Procedure
## Common Issues & Troubleshooting
## Maintenance & Updates
## Related Processes
```

**Best for**: How-to guides, operational procedures, oncall runbooks, recurring processes.

---

## Tips for Using These Scaffolds

- **Start simple**: Pick the scaffold that feels closest to your doc, then add or remove sections as needed.
- **Combine if needed**: Your document might blend sections from multiple scaffolds (e.g., a proposal that includes an ADR-style decision section).
- **Change names**: Rename sections to match your team's terminology (e.g., "Rationale" → "Why", "Consequences" → "Impact").
- **Stage 2 will help**: During refinement, we can adjust the outline together based on your content and feedback.

**Ready to start?** Pick a scaffold, begin filling in sections, and we'll refine it in Stage 2.
