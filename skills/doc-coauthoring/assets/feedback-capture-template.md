# How to Give Feedback During Stage 2 Refinement

During **Stage 2**, we'll work together to refine your document. This guide shows how to communicate feedback so we can iterate faster.

**The good news**: There's no single "right way" to give feedback. Use whatever style feels natural—here are common approaches:

---

## Feedback Styles (All Valid)

### 1. Numbered Selection
Pick which sections to keep, remove, or merge:

**Example:**
> "Keep sections 1, 3, 5, 7. Remove section 2 (duplicate of what's in the appendix). Combine sections 4 & 6—they're related. Add a new section on cost estimates."

**Why it works**: Crystal clear, easy to execute, minimal back-and-forth.

---

### 2. Freeform Feedback
React naturally to the draft:

**Example:**
> "Looks solid overall. The Problem section is too long—condense it to 2-3 paragraphs. The Goals are vague; make them more specific. Add a timeline to the Approach section."

**Why it works**: Conversational, focuses on the big picture, natural flow.

---

### 3. Specific Edits
Call out exact phrases and suggest changes:

**Example:**
> "In the Overview, change 'utilize' to 'use'. In Goals, change 'improve performance' to 'reduce latency by 50%'. After the 3rd paragraph, add a concrete cost example."

**Why it works**: Laser-focused, minimal interpretation needed, speeds up execution.

---

### 4. Direct Edits
Edit the document yourself; we'll learn from your changes:

**Example:**
> You directly revise sections, reorder paragraphs, or add content. Share the edited version, and we'll understand your preferences.

**Why it works**: The fastest path—no translation layer, your intent is clear from the diffs.

---

### 5. Mix & Match
Combine styles as needed:

**Example:**
> "Keep the structure, but rewrite the Problem section to be more concise. I've edited the Goals section directly (see attached). For the Approach, add a timeline and risk mitigation details."

**Why it works**: Realistic—different sections might need different feedback levels.

---

## How Each Style Speeds Up Iteration

| Feedback Style | Speed | Effort | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Numbered Selection** | Very Fast | Low | Big structural changes |
| **Freeform Feedback** | Fast | Low | General impressions, tone |
| **Specific Edits** | Fast | Medium | Targeted wording changes |
| **Direct Edits** | Very Fast | Medium | Hands-on refinement |
| **Mix & Match** | Medium | Low–Medium | Complex feedback |

---

## Our Process

1. **You share feedback** (any style you prefer)
2. **We execute** (pull together your input)
3. **You review** (check if we captured your intent)
4. **Iterate** until the doc feels right

---

## Tips

- **Be specific when it matters**: "Add examples" is harder to execute than "Add 2–3 concrete examples of X".
- **Vague feedback is okay too**: "Something feels off here" helps; we'll ask clarifying questions.
- **Preference patterns**: As we iterate, we'll learn your style and anticipate your needs for the next sections.
- **No rush**: Take your time reviewing. The more thoughtful your feedback, the better the final doc.

---

## Final Note

Whatever feedback style works for you is the right one. The more specific you can be, the faster we can learn your preferences and improve the next sections. **You're in control—just tell us what you're thinking.**
